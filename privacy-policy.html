Phone-MD — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 June 2026

Phone-MD is a markdown editor for iPhone. It is designed to be local-first: your documents and settings stay on your device or in the storage location you choose. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information.

Information we collect

None for analytics or advertising. Phone-MD does not contain analytics SDKs, advertising, or trackers, and the developer operates no servers that receive your content.

Your documents

Documents you create or open are stored on your device, in iCloud Drive, or in another file provider you select through the iOS Files system — under your control. Phone-MD does not transmit your documents to the developer.

On-device storage

The app stores, locally on your device only:

  • your list of recent documents (as security-scoped bookmarks);
  • app preferences (theme, font size, image-handling choice);
  • a temporary crash-recovery copy of unsaved edits.

Google Drive (optional)

If you choose to connect Google Drive, the app requests the drive.file permission, which grants access only to the files you explicitly open or create with Phone-MD — never your whole Drive. Authentication tokens are stored securely on your device (Apple Keychain). The developer does not receive, store, or transmit your Google account data or Drive files. Your use of Google Drive is also governed by Google's Privacy Policy. You can disconnect at any time from the app's settings, or revoke access at your Google Account permissions page.

Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your information with third parties. When you use the system Share sheet or export to HTML/PDF, the content goes only where you direct it.

Children's privacy

Phone-MD does not knowingly collect personal information from children and is not directed at children under 13.

Security

Data stays on your device or in your chosen storage provider, protected by iOS app sandboxing and (for credentials) the Apple Keychain.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy; material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above.
